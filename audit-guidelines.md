# Audit Report Guidelines

Guidelines based on corrections made during the Cremorne Point Manor Instagram audit. Apply to all future reports.

---

## Language & Tone

- Reports are written in **English**
- No em dashes (`—`) anywhere. Use a period, comma, or parentheses depending on context
- No creative or poetic section titles. Keep milestone and section names simple and direct
  - Bad: "The Discovery Paradox"
  - Good: "Good Visibility. No Engagement."

---

## Data & Accuracy

- **Never invent stats or use unsourced numbers.** If a figure can't be traced to a file, a platform, or a named report, remove it
  - Removed example: 10.83% CAGR — appeared in research HTML with no source cited
- **Verify all platform metrics before writing** (followers, engagement rate, post count, etc.)
  - Corrected example: Instagram had 3,497 followers, not "minimal presence"
- **Be precise about property-specific claims.** If a feature applies only to one room type, say so
  - Corrected example: only the penthouse has direct views of the Harbour Bridge and Opera House. Other rooms overlook Mosman Bay. Do not write "harbour views" as a blanket claim for the whole property
- Pricing comparisons must be contextually accurate. Do not compare prices across properties with different amenities or view access without noting the difference

---

## Scores & Coherence

- **Scores must reflect findings.** A section with a critical issue identified cannot score 100/100
  - Corrected example: Content had "only 12% Reels" flagged as a problem but scored 100/100. Adjusted to 72/100
- **All targets for the same timeframe must match across the entire document.** Check that roadmap sections, milestone cards, and KPI tables are aligned
  - Corrected example: Month 2–3 said "target 0.5%" but the 90-day milestone and KPI table both said 1.0%
- **The "most important action" must be consistent with the priorities stated throughout the report.** If Reels are the #1 format priority, the 7-day action must reflect that, not contradict it

---

## Research Intro Section

- Always include a "Based on Previous Research" block before the global score
- Include **specific competitor names** (not just "10 competitors analyzed")
- Only include stats that are directly traceable to the research files
- Suggested stats to consider: follower counts per platform, review count and satisfaction score, price range, competitor amenity gaps, and any relevant audience behavior data (e.g. 90% of brides use Instagram)

---

## Reels Analysis

- If video analysis data is available (e.g. `video_analysis_complete.json`), include a dedicated Reels section
- Always include: total views, avg rewatch rate, spoken CTA count, per-reel breakdown table
- Flag 0 spoken CTAs as critical — Instagram 2026 uses audio transcription for indexing
- Note which content type (view, promotional, reviews) performs best

---

## Conclusion Section

The conclusion is always the last section. It must include:

1. An integrated diagnosis paragraph (1–2 sentences)
2. Three summary metrics: what's Strong, what's Critical, what has Potential
3. "What is working" — green findings only
4. "What needs urgent change" — red/critical findings, ordered by impact
5. "The most valuable asset not being used" — specific to the property
6. "The most important action in the next 7 days" — must be consistent with the rest of the document's stated priorities
