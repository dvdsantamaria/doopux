Where the Site Stands Today

Done

The site was built with SEO foundations already included across all pages:

  • Structured metadata: tags in the <head> of each page telling Google what the page is about before it reads the content. Title, description, keywords. This is what appears in search results.
  • Canonical URLs: tell Google which is the official version of each page, preventing content accessible from multiple URLs from being penalised for duplication.
  • Open Graph tags: control how the site looks when shared on Facebook, LinkedIn or WhatsApp. Without these, the social network picks whatever it wants to show.
  • X cards: same as Open Graph but specific to X/Twitter.
  • Schema.org markup: structured language that tells Google not just what the page says, but what each thing is. For Northgate: business type, service areas, services offered, phone, email. Implemented with HomeAndConstructionBusiness and Service types per service area.
  • robots.txt: tells Google's crawlers which pages to index and which to ignore.
  • Performance-optimised assets: images in WebP format, compressed with lazy loading. A slow site ranks lower, especially on mobile.
  • Alt text: all WebP assets across every page have descriptive alt text.
  • Heading hierarchy: H1 tags confirmed per page. Index, Services and Projects pages had missing H1s, corrected during site handoff.
  • Title tags and meta descriptions: reviewed all website pages with keyword intent aligned to Sydney and Newcastle residential building.

Desktop is in excellent shape. SEO score is strong on both. The remaining opportunity is mobile performance, which directly affects Google's ranking signals since mobile-first indexing became the default. The foundation is done. What this proposal covers is connecting it to Google's measurement tools, closing the mobile performance gap, and then maintaining consistent upward pressure on rankings week by week.

SEO for AI Answers (Add-on)

Search behaviour is shifting. A growing share of homeowners now start their research not on Google, but by asking ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, or Google's AI Overviews a direct question. These models don't rank pages — they cite sources and surface answers. If Northgate isn't structured to be referenced by these systems, it's invisible to that audience.

llms.txt Implementation

  • Create and deploy a /llms.txt file, an emerging standard that signals to AI crawlers exactly what the site is, what it does, who it serves, and what content is authoritative
  • Analogous to robots.txt but built for AI agents, not search bots

Entity Clarity

  • Ensure Northgate Building Group is unambiguously defined as a named entity across all on-site content: name, location, services, service areas, credentials, and differentiators stated explicitly and consistently
  • LLMs build knowledge from patterns of consistent, factual statements. Ambiguity gets ignored.

Citeable Content Structure

  • Write or restructure key page sections as direct, factual answers to the questions AI models are likely to be asked about residential building in Sydney and Newcastle
  • Format content in ways LLMs can extract and cite cleanly: clear claims, attributed expertise, no filler
  • FAQ expansion on About and Services pages, both for Google's featured snippets and for AI answer surfaces

Schema Depth

  • Extend existing schema.org markup with additional types that LLMs weight: FAQPage, Review, Person (for team), Award
  • The site already has a 2025 award badge that should be marked up
  • Strengthen sameAs links to authoritative external sources

External Presence Signals

  • Identify and brief high-value directories, industry bodies, and editorial sources where Northgate should appear to be picked up by AI training and retrieval pipelines
  • LLMs learn from what's written about a business across the web, not just the business's own site
