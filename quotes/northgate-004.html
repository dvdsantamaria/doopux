Program Overview

Two Connected Pillars

This proposal covers two systems that reinforce each other. Workflow automations save hours every week by moving data between your tools without manual copying. SEO and AI search build the inbound channel so those saved hours translate into more qualified leads, not just less admin.

Both systems share one database. Every workflow knows what the other did because they all query the same source.

Phase 1 · Setup + Ongoing

Workflow Automations

n8n connects your existing tools — Outlook, GoHighLevel, Xero, SharePoint, Zoom — so data moves between them without anyone copying and pasting.

Recommended Starting Point

  • Meeting summaries. Fathom records your calls and transcribes them. n8n takes that transcript, sends it to an AI model for a structured summary, stores everything in a PostgreSQL database, and emails you the result. I recommend Fathom over Fireflies because the API is more complete and better documented.

This is the fastest way to prove value: every call becomes searchable, summarised, and stored automatically.

Automation Roadmap

  • Lead intake. Emails to leads@northgatebuilding.com.au get read automatically. n8n extracts the info, creates the contact in GHL, tags it, and notifies you. Specific actions go to specific addresses so the orchestrator knows exactly what to do. Accurate and controlled.
  • Variation drafts. Forward a client change request to variations@. n8n pulls the project from GHL and the contract from SharePoint, drafts the variation document, saves it as a draft in GHL, and flags it for your review. You add pricing and decide. The AI never contacts the client.
  • Progress claims. When a claim is ready in Xero, n8n drafts the client email and sends it to you for approval. Same with expense categorisation: receipts get read, categories get suggested, you bulk-approve weekly.
  • Document search. Eventually the system reads your SharePoint contracts and can answer questions about scope or flag differences between document versions.
Everything writes to the same database. One workflow knows what the other did because they all query the same source.

Phase 2 · Ongoing

SEO & AI Search

Northgate is a new business with no domain authority yet. You won't hit page 1 for "custom home builder Sydney" in three weeks. Anyone who tells you that is lying. What we can do is build the foundation properly and stack small wins every week. This isn't writing blog posts and hoping. There's actual technical work behind it.

Technical Monitoring Every Week

  • Search Console: what queries get impressions but no clicks, what pages have indexing errors, what Core Web Vitals are failing in reality.
  • CrUX (Chrome UX Report): how real users experience your site on real devices and real connections. A site can score 100 in Lighthouse and still be slow for actual visitors.
  • INP (Interaction to Next Paint): measures if the site responds when someone clicks "Get a Quote" or fills a form. If it lags, they leave. I track this and fix it.

Performance Optimisation Based on Field Data

  • TTFB: server response time from Australia. Every millisecond here multiplies downstream.
  • LCP: the main image or hero section loading fast enough that the user doesn't bounce.
  • Third-party script audit: that chat widget or analytics tool you installed might be killing your speed. I identify it and either lazy-load it or replace it.

Schema & Structured Data

  • LocalBusiness schema with specific service areas: Coogee, Mosman, Kiama — we will need to rank them first.
  • FAQPage schema for common questions.
  • HowTo schema for processes like "how to plan a knockdown rebuild".
  • BreadcrumbList for navigation clarity.
  • Review and AggregateRating schema once you have more client reviews.

Content Strategy — Conversion Pages

  • Service pages by location: "Custom Home Builder Coogee", "Knockdown Rebuild Bondi". These rank for local intent and convert better than generic homepages.
  • Comparison pages: e.g. "Knockdown rebuild vs renovation". The idea is to find leads that are already in decision mode.
  • Content gap analysis: what your competitors rank for that you don't, and building those pages.
  • Refresh cycle: existing content gets updated every 1–3 months so Google knows the site is active and with updated content.

AI Search

  • Perplexity, ChatGPT, Gemini: do they mention Northgate when someone asks "best builder Sydney" or "who builds custom homes in the Eastern Suburbs"?
  • llms.txt maintenance and updates as content grows.
  • Citations in directories and media that LLMs read and trust.

Local SEO

  • Google Business Profile: weekly posts, project photos, Q&A management.
  • Review strategy: how to ask, when to ask, how to respond to negative ones.
  • NAP consistency: your name, address, phone identical everywhere.
  • Citation building in Australian construction directories (Houzz, Hipages, TrueLocal, etc.).
  • Local pack optimisation: appearing in the map results when someone searches "builder near me".

Reporting

  • What I fixed technically.
  • What moved in Search Console.
  • What didn't move and why.
  • New content and conversion metrics for each one.
